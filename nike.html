Air Mag 'Back To The Future'

Immortalized in the 1989 film 'Back to the Future II,' the Nike Mag was finally released to the public in September 2011. Though the shoe lacks the self-lacing feature, it’s an aesthetic match to its filmic counterpart, complete with an electroluminescent Nike logo on the strap and glowing LED arrays at the midsole and heel. The 2011 production run was limited to 1500 pairs and released through charity eBay auctions where proceeds went directly to Michael J. Fox’s Foundation in finding a cure for Parkinson's Disease.

LeBron 15 'Equality' PE
$1000.00

TDistributed to lucky winners through a draw system, this new LeBron colorway was only released in a run of 200. The LeBron 15 'Equality' PE features a black BattleKnit upper with gold accents on the Swoosh, heel and large embroidered ‘EQUALITY’ lettering on the heel. The branding served as a nod to Nike’s “Equality” campaign to promote everyone — regardless of race and background — to stand up and speak for something. Each buyer was allowed to enter once per shoe size for free or unlimitedly with a $10 donation each time. These donations went to the Smithsonian National Museum of African American History and Culture.
